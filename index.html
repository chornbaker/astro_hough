AM207 Final Project

Applications of the Hough Transform for Detecting Moving Objects in Astronomical Data

About

This is the write-up for a Spring 2014 final project in AM 207 (Advanced Scientific Computing: Stochastic Optimization Methods. Monte Carlo Methods for Inference and Data Analysis) at Harvard University.

We examine applications of the following Hough Transform variations to detect moving objects in astronomical data:

  1. Standard Hough Transform
  2. Randomized Hough Transform
  3. 3D Hough Transform
  4. Probabilistic Hough Transform
  5. (2+2)D Hough Transform

Data and Results

We utilize a comination of synthetic data and a dataset from the Pan-STARRS survey consisting of over 300 epochs from 2010-2013. You can find out more about this data set and our results here.

C. Hornbaker, R. King, 2014
